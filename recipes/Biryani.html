Biryani

Description :

Biryani is a rich and aromatic one-pot dish made from rice, meat (chicken, beef, mutton, or fish), and a blend of aromatic spices. Its roots can be traced back to the Indian subcontinent, and it has spread to various parts of the world, especially with South Asian migration.

While its exact origins are debated, it is widely believed to have been brought to India by the Mughal emperors, who fused Persian cooking techniques with Indian ingredients.

Ingredients :

  • Basmati Rice-2 cups
  • Meat-500 grams
  • Yogurt-1/2 cup
  • Ginger-garlic paste – 1 tbsp
  • Red chili powder – 1-2 tsp
  • Cumin, coriander, garam masala – 1 tsp each
  • Turmeric powder – 1/2 tsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Lemon juice – 1 tbsp
  • Mint & cilantro – 1/4 cup each
  • Green chilies – 2-3
  • Whole spices (bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, star anise) – a few each
  • Saffron – a pinch (soaked in milk)
  • Fried onions – for garnish
  • Ghee/oil – 2 tbsp each
  • Water – 4 cups (for rice)

Preparations Steps :

  1. Marinate meat/veggies with yogurt, spices, lemon juice, mint, cilantro, and salt for at least 1 hour.
  2. Cook rice with whole spices (bay leaves, cardamom, etc.) until 70% done. Drain.
  3. Fry onions in ghee/oil until golden and crispy. Set aside.
  4. Layer cooked rice and marinated meat in a pot. Add saffron milk, mint, cilantro, and fried onions.
  5. Cook on low heat (Dum) for 30-40 minutes until fully cooked.
