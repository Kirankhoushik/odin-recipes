Sangati and Naatu Kodi Pulusu

Description

Ragi Sangati with Naatu Kodi Pulusu is a traditional Andhra meal combining soft, nutritious ragi rice balls with spicy country chicken curry. The mild flavor of the sangati balances the bold, rich taste of the pulusu, making it a hearty and wholesome dish loved across Andhra Pradesh.

Ingredients :

For Naatu Kodi :

  • 1 kg Naatu Kodi (country chicken, cleaned and cut into medium pieces)
  • 3 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 3 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 3–4 green chilies, slit
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2–3 tbsp red chili powder (adjust to spice level)
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp garam masala (or chicken masala powder)
  • A handful of fresh curry leaves
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 3 tbsp oil (groundnut oil or sunflower oil, traditional Andhra style uses sesame oil sometimes)
  • 2 cups water (adjust based on gravy thickness)
  • Salt — to taste

For Ragi Mudda :

  • 1 cup Ragi flour (finger millet flour)
  • ½ cup rice (raw rice or sona masuri, helps binding)
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 tsp salt

Steps :

Naatu Kodi Pulusu :

  1. Heat oil, add mustard, cumin, whole spices, dry chilies, curry leaves.
  2. Fry onions till golden → add ginger-garlic paste.
  3. Add tomatoes + green chilies, cook till soft.
  4. Mix in turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, salt.
  5. Add chicken pieces, coat well in masala, cook for 5–10 min.
  6. Add water, cover and simmer till chicken is tender.
  7. Finish with garam masala + coriander leaves.

Ragi Mudda

  1. Cook ½ cup rice in 3 cups water till very soft.
  2. Lower flame, add 1 cup ragi flour gradually.
  3. Stir continuously (no lumps) till smooth, thick, and non-sticky.
  4. Shape into balls with wet hands.
